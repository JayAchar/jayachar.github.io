I am a UK-trained medical doctor with a speciality in Infectious Diseases and Internal Medicine from Australia. I have worked for MSF UK since 2014 as a tuberculosis (TB), HIV and viral hepatitis specialist. Aside from an emergency secondment to support the organisation during the West African Ebola outbreak, my operational attention has been predominantly on drug-resistant tuberculosis and the Eastern European and Central Asian countries of the WHO Euro region. Since the beginning of 2019, I have also led MSF’s technical TB working group - a role that has required a greater focus on exernal relations, advocacy and global policy and research.

I’m passionate about access to healthcare, people-centredness and the bioethical concepts of equity and justice. In the field of TB, I work hard to improve our knowledge on the epidemiology, diagnosis and treatment of drug-resistant TB.

Aside from my clinical and epidemiological interests in TB, I am also improving my skills in data processing, analysis and visualisation. This has been a natural progression from my MSc and the frequent use of programmatic data in my current roles.

In addition to my medical degree from University College London, I am also a Fellow of the Royal Australasian College of Physicians. I was awarded a Diploma of Tropical Medicine and Hygiene (DTM&H) by the Royal College of Physicians and have completed a MSc in Epidmiology at the London School of Hygiene and Tropical Medicine.
