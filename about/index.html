About

What do I do?

My name is Jay Achar. I am a UK-trained medical doctor with a speciality in Infectious Diseases and Internal Medicine from Australia. I have worked for MSF UK since 2014 as a tuberculosis (TB), HIV and viral hepatitis specialist. Aside from an emergency secondment to support the organisation during the West African Ebola outbreak, my operational attention has been predominantly on drug-resistant tuberculosis and the Eastern European and Central Asian countries of the WHO Euro region.

I’m passionate about access to healthcare, people-centredness and the bioethical concepts of equity and justice. In the field of TB, I work hard to improve our knowledge on the epidemiology, diagnosis and treatment of drug-resistant TB.

In addition to my medical degree from University College London, I am also a Fellow of the Royal Australasian College of Physicians. I was awarded a Diploma of Tropical Medicine and Hygiene (DTM&H) by the Royal College of Physicians and have completed a MSc in Epidmiology at the London School of Hygiene and Tropical Medicine.
